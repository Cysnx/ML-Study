Data→ Any observations that have been collected.

Statistics→ Collecting, analyzing, summarizing, interpreting, and drawing conclusions from DATA.

Population→ The complete set of elements being studied.

Sample → Some subset of a population.

Census→Collecting from every member of a population.

** If you take a sample, it must be collected randomly.



Types of Data:

Parameter→ A characteristic of a population. P-P

Statistic→ A characteristic of a sample. S-S

Two Types of Data:

Qualitative/Categorical→Non- Numeric: Color, Gender, Race, Religion, Zipcode*, Phone number *, etc.

*Mathematical operations are meaningless.

Quantitative→ Numerical; Height/ Weight, KPH, Wage, Time, etc.

* Mathematical operations are meaningful.

Two Types of Quantitative Data:

Discrete Data: Countable or finite: # of eggs, dice, etc.

 * Usually a ‘count’.

Continuous Data: Infinite number of possible values (not countable): Temp, etc.

* Usually a ‘measurement’.



4 Levels of Measurement:

Nominal: Categories NOT ordered: Religion, gender, etc.

Ordinal: Can be ordered.

Differences are meaningless. e.g: Rank, color (spectrum).

Interval: Ordered, differences are meaningful. But no 'natural zero': Temperature (except Kelvin).

Ratio: Just like interval but w/ ‘natural zero’. e.g: Amount of Money.
